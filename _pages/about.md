Dr. Jorge Valverde-Rebaza is a researcher and consultant in Artificial Intelligence with over a decade of experience at the intersection of science, technology, and real-world impact. He holds a Master’s (2013) and Ph.D. (2017) in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics from the University of São Paulo (USP), enriched by international research internships in Portugal (2014) and France (2015).

His research interests center on Natural Language Processing (NLP), Large Language Models (LLMs), multi-agent systems, Generative AI, Deep Learning, Machine Learning, and Complex Networks. He explores both theoretical foundations and applied methodologies, advancing solutions that integrate reasoning strategies, retrieval-augmented generation, and agentic workflows with memory, planning, and human-in-the-loop mechanisms.

Dr. Valverde-Rebaza’s contributions span both academic and applied fronts. He co-designed an award-winning clinical imaging system for COVID-19 CT-scan analysis that supported decision-making in hospital environments, and an HR analytics platform later integrated into large-scale corporate operations. His research on Complex Networks has provided novel insights into modeling social, urban, and information systems, while his applied work with LLMs and GenAI has delivered production-ready solutions for business intelligence, education, and human resources. He has also pioneered privacy-preserving methods for LLM adoption in the public sector, a contribution recognized with a Best Paper Award, reinforcing his commitment to ethical, secure, and socially impactful AI.

He combines strong technical skills—ranging from Python, PyTorch, and LangChain to AWS-based MLOps—with a strategic perspective on experimentation, governance, and adoption frameworks. Passionate about knowledge transfer, he actively mentors emerging talent, supervises research projects, and publishes applied studies, seeking to bridge academic rigor with industry transformation and to position AI as a driver of innovation and social good.
