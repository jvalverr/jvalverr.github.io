# Dr. Jorge Valverde-Rebaza – Artificial Intelligence Researcher and Professor

Dr. Jorge Valverde-Rebaza is a researcher and consultant in **Artificial Intelligence** with more than a decade of experience working at the intersection of scientific research, technological innovation, and real-world applications.

He holds a **Ph.D. in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics (2017)** and a **Master’s degree (2013)** from the **University of São Paulo (USP)**. During his doctoral training, he conducted research visits at the **University of Porto (Portugal)** and **Université de Montpellier (France)**.

Currently, he is a **Professor at the School of Engineering and Sciences at Tecnológico de Monterrey** and has collaborated with academic institutions and research groups across **Peru, Brazil, and Mexico**.

## Research Areas

His research focuses on several topics in **Artificial Intelligence and Data Science**, including:

- Natural Language Processing (NLP)
- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- Generative Artificial Intelligence
- Machine Learning and Deep Learning
- Multi-agent systems
- Complex Networks
- Data-driven decision systems

His work combines **theoretical foundations and applied research**, developing intelligent systems that integrate reasoning strategies, retrieval-augmented generation, and agent-based architectures.

---

## Research Contributions

Dr. Valverde-Rebaza’s work spans both **academic research and real-world applications**.

Some notable contributions include:

- Development of **AI-based clinical imaging systems for COVID-19 CT scan analysis** used to support hospital decision-making.
- Design of **HR analytics platforms** later adopted in large-scale corporate environments.
- Research on **complex networks** to model social, urban, and information systems.
- Development of **privacy-preserving methods for Large Language Models**, recognized with a **Best Paper Award**.

His work also explores **LLM-based solutions for business intelligence, education, and public sector innovation**.
