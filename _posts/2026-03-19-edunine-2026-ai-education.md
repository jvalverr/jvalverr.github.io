I recently participated in **EDUNINE 2026** at Tecnológico de Monterrey Mexico City campus, where one idea became very clear: AI is no longer something we prepare for in education — it's already reshaping it.

## What stood out

Across discussions, there was a strong focus on how engineering and computer science programs must evolve to integrate AI not as an add-on, but as a **core component**. At the same time, conversations around ethics, responsibility, and real-world impact — especially in LATAM — are becoming central, not optional.

What I found particularly valuable is how the region is contributing its own perspective, grounded in practical challenges and opportunities that differ from more mature ecosystems.

## Beyond the sessions

The most important part was connecting with researchers from around the world working at the intersection of AI and education, including Shaimaa Ali, Agatha Clarice da Silva Ovando, Arturo Molina-Cristóbal, Nicolas Sumba, Joao Leopoldo e Silva, Jonathan Loo, Elio San Cristobal Ruiz, and Roberto Portillo, among others.

Special thanks to Martin Molina Espinosa for the excellent local organization.

Those exchanges are what truly move the field forward.
