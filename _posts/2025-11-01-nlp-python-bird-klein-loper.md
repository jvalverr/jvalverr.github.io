Some books age, others become timeless — and *Natural Language Processing with Python* by Steven Bird, Ewan Klein & Edward Loper is definitely the latter.

This book isn't about the latest LLMs or GenAI trends. It's about **the foundations that made today's language models possible.**

## What makes it special

What sets this book apart is how it teaches you to think like an NLP engineer: tokenizing, tagging, parsing, and building simple models step by step, with working Python code. It's open, clear, and still incredibly useful for anyone starting in AI or data science.

For anyone wanting to understand how LLMs evolved from early NLP systems, this book remains a perfect starting point.

> Sometimes, revisiting the basics is the fastest way to master what's next.
