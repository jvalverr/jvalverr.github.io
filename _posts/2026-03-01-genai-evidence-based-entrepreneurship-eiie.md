Last November, I had the opportunity to present my research at the **VIII International Research Meeting in Entrepreneurship (VIII EIIE)**. My contribution tackled a question that still matters to me:

> Why do so many early-stage ventures fail, even when founders follow established frameworks?

## The real problem is upstream

In many cases, the issue isn't execution — it's upstream. Problems are often poorly quantified, and validation relies too heavily on what people say instead of what they do.

## How Generative AI can help

In this research, Nathalia C. and I explore how Generative AI (especially LLMs) can be integrated into the earliest stages of venture creation to strengthen both sides:

**As an evidence aggregator** — synthesizing large volumes of unstructured signals (reviews, social discourse, patents, domain repositories) to surface recurring pains and frame problems with more rigor.

**As a validation engine** — generating realistic artifacts and scenarios that enable low-cost micro-experiments designed to capture behavioral commitment, not just stated interest.

Importantly, the goal is not to replace entrepreneurial intuition, but to complement it with more structured, evidence-based reasoning and stronger validation signals.
