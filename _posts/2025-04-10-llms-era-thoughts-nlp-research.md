We are clearly in an **LLM era**. Progress in **Natural Language Processing** is not just about incremental benchmarks — it is about how **large language models** change the questions we ask, the methods we trust, and what “research” means when the default starting point is often a foundation model rather than training from scratch.

This note points to a longer write-up on that shift: the impact of LLMs on the **NLP research agenda**, tensions between emergent capability and rigorous evaluation, and where the field may head as generative models keep expanding what language technology can do.

## In brief

- How **large-scale pre-training** shifted the center of gravity in NLP — from bespoke task models to **generalist** language models
- What changes when **emergent behaviors** and **in-context learning** sit alongside classical supervised pipelines
- **Trade-offs** between impressive demos, solid **evaluation**, and **reproducibility** as models grow
- Where **NLP research** may head when the default stack is generative, API-driven, and constantly moving
