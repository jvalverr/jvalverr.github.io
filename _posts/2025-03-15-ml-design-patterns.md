Most ML conversations still revolve around models. But production success depends on something else: design patterns.

*Machine Learning Design Patterns* isn't about chasing the latest architecture. It's about understanding the recurring challenges that emerge when ML systems move from notebooks to real-world environments, and how to address them systematically.

The book explores reusable patterns for:

- Data pipelines
- Separating training from inference
- Monitoring and retraining loops
- Managing data drift

What I value most is how it shifts the mindset from **"Does the model work?"** to **"Will the system endure?"**

> Prototypes are easy. Durable systems are not.

At a time when GenAI and agents dominate the headlines, this perspective feels essential. If you're building AI that needs to scale, integrate, and last beyond the demo phase, patterns aren't optional: they're foundational.
